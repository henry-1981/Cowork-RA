## 1. Gifts/Benefits - General Prohibition

**Code Art. 5 / Operating Standard Art. 2**

### Rule
- Provision of gifts/benefits (금품류) to medical institutions or HCPs is PROHIBITED
- Exception: Only items explicitly permitted under Articles 6-17, within socially accepted business customs

### Definition of "gifts/benefits" (금품류)
- Goods, equipment, real estate (including usage rights)
- Cash, deposit certificates, gift certificates, securities, payment IOUs
- Entertainment (meals, performances, sports, travel, golf, ski invitations)
- Transportation, accommodation, conference registration, labor/services
- Discounts, surcharges, sales incentives (except statutory cost discounts and credit card reward points)

### Indirect Provision Rules
- Provision through subsidiaries, affiliates, distributors, or marketing agencies = treated as company's own provision
- Provision to HCP's family, relatives, or special-relationship entities = treated as provision to the HCP

## 2. Samples

**Code Art. 6 / Operating Standard Art. 3**

### Demonstration Samples (시연용)
- **Purpose**: Improve HCP/patient awareness
- **Labeling**: Must mark "Not for human use" (인체사용금지)
- **Recipient**: Medical institution (by relevant department)
- **Quantity**: 1-2 units in minimum packaging
- **Duration**: Minimum necessary period

### Evaluation Samples (평가용)
- **Purpose**: Evaluate proper use and function for purchase decision
- **Labeling**: Must mark "Cannot bill patient separately"
- **Recipient**: Medical institution (by relevant department)
- **Quantity**: 1-2 units or minimum quantity for evaluation
- **Duration**: Maximum 1 month for repeated-use devices
- **Written agreement**: Evaluation conditions must be documented in advance
- **Ownership**: Remains with the company during evaluation
- **Return**: Must retrieve immediately after evaluation unless purchased/leased
- **Additional provision**: 1 additional allowed only for significant product changes or trial-before-purchase products (e.g., contact lenses)

### Prohibited
- Provision to individual HCPs NOT affiliated with a medical institution
- Provision to non-HCP staff (코디네이터, 구매과) individually (only to institution)

## 3. Donations

**Code Art. 7 / Operating Standard Art. 4**

### Charitable Donations (자선 목적)
- **Target**: Medical institutions, medical/device research organizations for charitable activities
- **Approval**: Pre-report to KMDIA at least 10 days before donation
- **Post-report**: Within 10 days after delivery, submit proof to KMDIA
- **Review fee**: Exempt
- **No monetary cap** in regulation, but large amounts or equipment require careful review

### Designated Donations (지정기부)
- **Target**: Academic awards, campaigns (NOT research funding)
- **Procedure**: Institution requests KMDIA → KMDIA posts 15-day collection notice → Companies apply → Review committee selects → Direct donation
- **Review fee**: 1.5% of donation (members), 3% (non-members), VAT excluded
- **Caps**: Campaign max 50M KRW; Academic award max 5M KRW per person
- **Timeline**: Request at least 3 months before project start

### Prohibited Donations
- Donations linked to device adoption/purchase decisions
- Funding for real estate, facilities, or operating capital that institutions should bear themselves
- Repeated/continuous donations to the same organization without justification
- Exposing company name/brand on jointly hosted or donation-funded events (disease campaigns, awards)

### Committee Guidance (2024 revision)
- **Investigator-initiated clinical trials**: NOT eligible as donations. Results belong to the researcher, making it difficult to view as compensation for HCP services. May constitute "economic benefits for sales promotion purposes." Must follow Medical Device Distribution Order Maintenance Rules [Annex 2] for clinical trial "support" instead
- **Academic award naming**: Award name must NOT directly or indirectly reference any specific company or product name
- **Support request deadline violations**: Accumulating 3+ warnings per year for deadline non-compliance may result in exclusion from support eligibility for 3, 6, 9, or 12 months. Valid reasons must be submitted in a formal reason report

## 4. Conference Hosting Support

**Code Art. 8 / Operating Standard Art. 5**

### Domestic Conference Support
- **Target**: Conferences hosted by approved medical/academic organizations
- **Support methods**: F&B, souvenirs, booth rental, advertising, etc.
- **Self-funding ratio**: Conference organizer must self-fund at least 30%
- **Review fee**: 1.5% of support amount (cap 1.5M KRW for amounts over 100M), non-members 3%
- **Timeline**: Request 2 quarters before event; Company applies within 15-day notice period
- **Post-report**: Company reports within 1 month; Organizer submits cost settlement within 3 months

### International Conference (Domestic Venue)
- Requirements: Participants from 5+ countries, or 150+ foreign participants with 2+ day duration
- Must be recognized by medical associations or KMDIA

### Prohibited
- Company involvement in conference themes, proceedings, participant selection, or materials
- Direct payment to speakers/chairs at domestic conferences (must go through organizing body)

### In-Kind Medical Device/Facility Support at Conferences (2024 revision)

Per Ministry of Health and Welfare interpretation (약무정책과-3096), in-kind device/facility support is permitted if:
- Support is part of conference program content for academic purpose (device demonstration, surgical skills training)
- Does NOT directly influence HCPs' medical device selection
- Remaining devices/facilities MUST be returned after the conference
- Society self-funding ratio of 30% applies: device/facility value cannot exceed 70% of total conference budget

**Domestic conference procedure:**
1. Society submits: business plan, total budget, requested device/facility list
2. Company submits: device/facility support application list with pricing
   - Reimbursed devices: Insurance upper limit price
   - Non-reimbursed devices: Fair market supply price
   - Equipment/facilities (temporary use): Fair market rental rate

**International conference (domestic venue) procedure:**
1. Pre-notification: society's request letter, support application list, company-society support contract (must include Code compliance and surplus return clauses)
2. Post-notification: society's receipt for devices, confirmation of facility use, actual support list

## 5. Conference Attendance Support

**Code Art. 9 / Operating Standard Art. 6**

### Domestic Conference Attendance
- **Eligible**: HCPs selected as presenters, chairs, or discussants by the conference organizer
- **Support scope**: Registration, transportation, accommodation, F&B within limits
- **Accommodation**: Per operating standard limits
- **Review fee**: 1.5% (members), 3% (non-members)

### Overseas Conference Attendance
- **Eligible**: HCPs selected by international conference organizer with delegation authority to domestic academic body
- **Support scope**: Registration, economy class airfare, accommodation, F&B
- **Required**: KMDIA prior review

### Prohibited
- Supporting HCPs attending as general audience (not presenters/chairs/discussants)
- Korean company funding for overseas training hosted by parent company

### Presenter Support Limits (2024 revision)

**Video Session presenters:**
- Support ONLY if: abstract accepted, presentation/Q&A time specified, presenter role confirmed
- Only 1 person among lead author or co-authors who actually participates in the presentation

**Oral Presentation presenters:**
- Support for up to 2 persons total:
  - Lead author (first author or corresponding author) - MUST be included
  - Plus 1 co-author
- Lead author must be one of the 2 supported persons

## 6. Product Presentations

**Code Art. 10 / Operating Standard Art. 7**

### Multi-Institution Presentations (복수기관 대상)
- **F&B limit**: Max 100,000 KRW per person per meal (tax/service charge excluded, refreshments included)
- **Multi-day with F&B**: Max 3 meals/day, max 150,000 KRW/day total
- **Transportation**: Economy class domestic airfare, KTX, express bus, or equivalent public transport
- **Taxi**: Exceptionally max 30,000 KRW with documented justification
- **Accommodation**: Prior KMDIA approval required; within operating standard limits
- **Promotional items**: Max 10,000 KRW per item
- **Audience**: At least 10 HCPs
- **Prior approval**: Required for presentations with accommodation

### Single-Institution Presentations (개별기관 방문)
- **F&B limit**: Max 100,000 KRW per person per meal (tax/service charge excluded, refreshments included)
- **Frequency limit**: Max 4 times per month per company per institution (F&B-providing visits)
- **Promotional items**: Max 10,000 KRW per item
- **Accommodation**: NOT permitted
- **No audience minimum**

### Prohibited
- Presentations outside Korea with F&B
- Non-product topics (tax, legal, management education)
- Providing F&B at times not adjacent to the presentation
- Driver service fees (대리운전비)
- Coupons usable beyond meal service (e.g., hotel gift shop)
- Supporting companions/accompanying persons of HCPs (only HCPs and directly related procedure/diagnostic personnel eligible)

### Committee Guidance for Product Presentations (2024 revision)

**Receipt interpretation:**
- "1 receipt per meal" includes: (1) split-payment receipts from same dining location at adjacent times, (2) refreshment/beverage receipts from times adjacent to the meal

**Venue restrictions:**
- Events at Jeju Island, resorts, or locations susceptible to entertainment suspicion require additional documentation: detailed event plan, reason statement, confirmation letter
- Committee conducts thorough review for such venues

**Taxi policy (detailed):**
- Taxis are NOT public transportation
- Exceptionally allowed with unavoidable reason only: max 30,000 KRW per event, actual expense basis
- Must submit reason statement reviewed by Committee
- Valid example: Late clinic hours making public transport unavailable
- Invalid example: Taxi use at 18:00-19:00 when public transport still operates (rejected by Committee in actual case)

**Local transportation:**
- Max 150,000 KRW per person (applying Art. 6(5)(5) conference attendance rules by analogy)
- Exceeding 150,000 KRW: must submit reason statement for Committee review

**Pre-approval for accommodation events:**
- MUST obtain Committee approval BEFORE hosting events with accommodation
- Application deadline: 40 days before event date

**Simple change exemptions (re-review criteria):**

| Change Type | Re-review Required? | Details |
|-------------|-------------------|---------|
| Venue | YES | Venue change always requires re-review |
| Date | Conditional | Simple date change: EXEMPT (must pre-report). Year change: re-review required |
| Agenda | YES | Agenda change: re-review. Sub-topic/speaker changes within agenda: EXEMPT (must pre-report) |
| Attendees increase | Conditional | 10%+ increase: re-review required |
| Attendees decrease | NO | Exempt from re-review |
| Budget increase (per-person) | YES | Any increase in per-person economic benefits: re-review |
| Budget decrease (per-person) | NO | Exempt from re-review |

**Partial attendance:**
- Support available for HCPs with socially acceptable reasons for partial attendance
- HCPs who partially attend due to personal reasons may NOT re-participate in the same program
- Illness, disaster, ceremonial occasions are always accepted as valid reasons

**Budget reporting:**
- Report ONLY economic benefits provided to HCPs (exclude company employee costs)
- If post-event settlement includes employee costs: must additionally itemize HCP vs. employee costs separately

## 7. Education/Training

**Code Art. 11 / Operating Standard Art. 8**

### Domestic Education/Training
- **Purpose**: Skills acquisition/improvement for procedures and diagnostics directly related to the medical device
- **F&B limit**: Same as product presentations (100,000 KRW/person/meal)
- **No audience minimum** (unlike product presentations)
- **Prior approval**: Required for multi-institution training with accommodation

### Overseas Education/Training
- **Eligible**: For devices not yet imported to Korea, or where domestic training is infeasible
- **Support**: Economy class airfare, accommodation within limits
- **Prior KMDIA review**: Required
- **1st-class devices**: Strict justification required for overseas training necessity

### Prohibited
- Single-institution training with accommodation at external venues
- Co-hosting with hospitals (company must be sole organizer in substance)
- Supporting companions/accompanying persons of HCPs

### Committee Guidance for Education/Training (2024 revision)

**Overseas training justification:**
- Products with 5+ years since initial approval/certification: Must submit reason statement with necessity explanation and planned launch date
- Simple model number additions/changes: Must submit comparison table (differences from existing product, surgical method differences), previous training history, and reason for overseas training necessity

**Class 1 device overseas training:**
- May be DENIED even for products not yet imported if valid necessity cannot be confirmed
- When necessity is ambiguous: Committee consults with relevant academic societies

**Receipt, venue, taxi, local transportation, simple change, partial attendance, and budget reporting rules:** Same as Product Presentations Committee Guidance above

## 8. Lectures/Consulting

**Code Art. 12 / Operating Standard Art. 9**

### Lecture Fees (강연료)
| Duration | Maximum Fee |
|----------|------------|
| 40 min+ | 500,000 KRW |
| 1 hour+ | 500,000 KRW |
| Per additional hour | 500,000 KRW |
- Fees proportional to actual lecture time for durations under 40 minutes
- **Annual cap per HCP**: 3,000,000 KRW (including parent company payments)

### Consulting Fees (자문료)
| Type | Maximum Fee |
|------|------------|
| Written advisory (서면자문) | 200,000 KRW per case |
| Meeting advisory (회의자문, 2hr+) | 500,000 KRW per session |
| Per additional hour | 250,000 KRW |
- **Annual cap per HCP**: 3,000,000 KRW

### Chair Fees (좌장비)
- Same as lecture/consulting fees based on role
- Must document chair's substantive contribution

### Additional Expenses
- Transportation, accommodation, F&B: Actual expenses within product presentation limits
- Public officials: Must follow government travel expense regulations

### Required Documentation
- Objective selection criteria for lecturer/consultant
- Minimum necessary number of persons
- Detailed contract with scope of work
- Evidence of work completion
- Records of selection rationale, dates, content, and utilization

### Prohibited
- Repeated engagement of same HCP for same/similar topic without justification
- Payment of full fee before completion
- Lectures unrelated to medical device science/education

## 10. Market Research

**Code Art. 14 / Operating Standard Art. 10**

### Compensation Limits
- **Surveys under 30 min**: Max 50,000 KRW per respondent
- **Surveys 30 min+**: Compensation proportional to time invested
- **Expert research**: Consulting fee standards apply (Art. 12)

### Requirements
- Must be conducted by professional market research organization or have documented internal standards
- Prior notification to KMDIA required
- Results must be used for legitimate business purposes
- Patient surveys: Possible, but must not involve HCPs as intermediaries for compensation

## 11. Post-Market Surveillance

**Code Art. 15 / Operating Standard Art. 11**

- **Case report fee**: Max 50,000 KRW per case report
- **Number of cases**: Minimum required by Medical Devices Act
- Must follow MFDS post-market surveillance regulations
- Prior notification and post-report to KMDIA required

## 12. Other Clinical Activities

**Code Art. 16 / Operating Standard Art. 12**

- Covers clinical activities beyond PMS (e.g., investigator-initiated studies, registries)
- Must have clear scientific/educational purpose
- Compensation based on actual work, within consulting fee standards
- Prior KMDIA review required
- Post-report required

## 13. Exhibition/Advertising

**Code Art. 17 / Operating Standard Art. 13**

### Exhibition Booths
- Permitted at conferences with KMDIA conference support approval
- **Booth rental**: Max 5,000,000 KRW per booth (VAT excluded)
- **Booth area**: Max 12 sqm
- **Promotional items at booth**: Max 10,000 KRW per item

### Advertising
- Print/digital advertising in conference materials
- Must comply with Medical Devices Act advertising regulations
- **Ad cost**: Reported as part of conference support

### Prohibited
- Product demonstrations at conference session venues (only at booths)
- Exhibition at events without KMDIA conference support approval

### Committee Guidance for Exhibition/Advertising (2024 revision)

**Quarterly reporting:** Companies must report exhibition/advertising activities to KMDIA quarterly using prescribed forms

**Eligible advertising media for payments to medical institutions:**
- Print materials or equivalent electronic documents created for disease treatment, prevention, or education
- Must be distributed/displayed to multiple HCPs at multiple medical institutions

**Electronic document rules:**
- Content and format must be equivalent to paper print materials or educational materials
- Cannot pay advertising fees for documents that simply repeat or minimally modify existing content
- If both print and electronic versions exist: only print advertising costs may be paid

**Conference app advertising:**
- Recognized as eligible for advertising payment ONLY when no print media advertising exists at the conference
- Payment standard follows electronic document criteria

## 14. Tax/Fee Standards

**Operating Standard Art. 22**

### VAT Treatment
- **VAT excluded**: Review fees for donations (Art. 4-4), conference support (Art. 5-2), attendance support (Art. 6-7), product presentations (Art. 7-3), education/training (Art. 8-5), exhibition/advertising (Art. 13)
- **VAT and service charge excluded, refreshments included**: Product presentation F&B (Art. 7-1-3), education/training F&B (Art. 8-1-3)
- **Tax included**: Lecture/consulting fees (Art. 9-1, 9-3), market research compensation (Art. 10-1-4)

## Penalties Summary

**Code Art. 20 / Operating Standard Art. 18**

- Warning (경고)
- Corrective order (시정명령)
- Publication of violation (위반사실 공표)
- Exclusion from KMDIA activities
- Fine: Up to 200% of the violation amount
- Referral to Fair Trade Commission for investigation

### Committee Guidance for Penalties (2024 revision)

**Warning issuance rules:**
- 1 warning per review case per violation occurrence
- Multiple violations in a single review case: Committee may escalate to minor penalty (경징계) or major penalty (중징계) based on severity
- Support request deadline violations: 3+ accumulated warnings per year may result in exclusion from support eligibility for 3, 6, 9, or 12 months
