## Chapter 1: General Provisions

### Article 1 (Purpose)

This Fair Competition Code for Medical Device Transactions (hereinafter "the Code") aims to establish fair competition order in medical device distribution and maintain/improve public health by discouraging unfair customer solicitation as regulated under the Monopoly Regulation and Fair Trade Act (hereinafter "MRFTA") Article 23, Paragraph 1, Item 3.

### Article 2 (Basic Principles)

Companies and distributors shall comply with the following basic principles:
1. Marketing activities for medical devices must be conducted within the scope of the MRFTA and other relevant laws, and within socially accepted normal business customs
2. Companies and distributors shall deliver scientific and educational product information to HCPs and maximize patient benefits. However, such efforts shall NOT compromise HCPs' independence in selecting medical devices for clinical use
3. Activities under Paragraph 2 shall take place at appropriate venues matching their purpose
4. All accounting records and financial management shall be recorded and managed accurately and transparently based on facts, in compliance with relevant laws and generally accepted accounting principles

### Article 3 (Definitions)

1. **Medical device (의료기기)**: As defined in Medical Devices Act Article 2(1)
2. **Manufacturer/Importer (사업자)**: Entities with manufacturing or import licenses under Medical Devices Act Articles 6 or 15
3. **Repair business (수리업자)**: Entities registered for medical device repair under Medical Devices Act Article 16
4. **Distributor (판매업자 등)**: Entities registered for sales or rental under Medical Devices Act Article 17
5. **Medical institution (의료기관)**: As defined in Medical Act Article 3
6. **Healthcare professional (보건의료인)**: Persons qualified, licensed, or permitted to provide healthcare services per Framework Act on Health and Medical Services Article 3(3). Excludes pharmacists and herbal pharmacists EXCEPT those working at medical institutions (who are treated as HCPs under the Code)
   - Medical Act: physicians, dentists, oriental medicine doctors, midwives, nurses
   - Medical Technicians Act: clinical pathologists, radiologists, physical therapists, occupational therapists, dental technicians, dental hygienists, medical records technicians, opticians
   - Other laws: sanitarians, paramedics (Grade 1/2), prosthetists/orthotists, health educators, medical assistants, nurse aides, bone-setters, acupuncturists, moxibustionists, hospital-employed pharmacists
   - Must be institution-affiliated (개설자 or employee)
   - Healthcare service workers (non-specific roles like cleaning, security excluded)
7. **Foreign HCP (해외보건의료인)**: Medical professionals recognized in countries other than Korea. NOT covered by the Code UNLESS affiliated with a domestic medical institution
8. **Samples (견본품)**: Complete products for demonstration purposes
9. **Donation (기부행위)**: Gratuitous provision of goods/money to medical institutions, schools, academic/research organizations regardless of designation (welcome gifts, sponsorship, charitable contributions, etc.)
10. **Academic conference (학술대회)**: Events providing scientific/educational medical information to HCPs (conferences, symposiums, seminars, etc.), EXCLUDING company-hosted events
    - Domestic international conference: Participants from 5+ countries (HCPs attending as audience from 5+ countries, or 150+ foreign attendees, 2+ days), recognized by medical associations or KMDIA
    - International society: 100+ foreign full members from 5+ countries, recognized by medical associations or KMDIA
11. **Product presentation (제품설명회)**: Company-hosted events providing information about own medical devices to HCPs at multiple or individual medical institutions
12. **Education/Training (교육·훈련)**: Company-conducted activities for HCPs and procedure/diagnostic personnel, conveying information directly related to medical technology use, or training for safe and effective device use
13. **Market research (시장조사)**: Activities collecting data on market size, characteristics, and consumer needs
14. **Post-market surveillance (시판후조사)**: Safety and efficacy surveys conducted per Medical Devices Act Article 8
15. **Gifts/benefits (금품류)**: Any items of economic value provided to medical institutions or HCPs:
    - Goods, equipment, real estate, structures (including usage rights)
    - Cash, deposit certificates, gift cards, securities, payment undertakings
    - Entertainment (meals, performances, sports, travel, golf, ski invitations)
    - Transportation, accommodation, conference registration, other conveniences
    - Labor and other services
    - Discounts, surcharges, sales incentives (EXCEPT statutory cost discounts and credit card reward points)
16. **Electronic documents (전자문서)**: As defined in Electronic Documents and Transactions Act Article 2(1)

### Article 4 (Operating Standards)

1. KMDIA may establish detailed operating standards for the Code
2. The Fair Trade Commission may recommend establishment or amendment of operating standards

## Chapter 2: Scope of Permitted Benefits

### Article 5 (Restriction on Provision of Gifts/Benefits)

1. Companies and distributors shall NOT provide gifts/benefits to medical institutions or HCPs, and shall NOT comply with requests for such provision. EXCEPTION: Provisions under Articles 6-17 that fall within socially accepted normal business customs
2. (Reserved for Code provisions)
3. The following are treated as the company's own provision:
   - Providing gifts through domestic/overseas headquarters, affiliates, distributors, or marketing agencies for delivery to medical institutions/HCPs
   - Providing gifts to distributors/marketing agencies knowing (or having reason to know) they would be delivered to medical institutions/HCPs
4. Provision to HCP's family, relatives, or special-relationship persons/entities is treated as provision to the HCP/institution

### Article 6 (Provision of Samples)

1. Companies may provide medical devices marked as "samples" to medical institutions for product evaluation, in minimum packaging units/quantities/periods
2. Samples may be categorized as demonstration or evaluation samples with respective rules (see Operating Standard Article 3 for details)

### Article 7 (Donations)

1. Permitted donation activities within socially accepted scope:
   - Medical, educational, or charitable purpose donations to academic/research organizations
   - Academic awards, campaigns through KMDIA-approved process
   - Charitable donations of medical devices to medical institutions (with KMDIA pre-notification)
2. Prohibited: Donations linked to device adoption; funding institution's own operating costs; repeated donations without justification
3. KMDIA verifies proper use of donated funds
4. Donations to academic/research organizations must follow KMDIA review committee procedures

### Article 8 (Conference Hosting/Operating Support)

1. Companies may support conferences hosted by approved medical/academic organizations
2. Conference organizer self-funding ratio: at least 30%
3. Domestic conference support: F&B, souvenirs, booth rental, advertising within limits
4. International conference (domestic venue): Enhanced scope per operating standards
5. Companies shall NOT involve themselves in conference themes, proceedings, participant selection, or materials
6. Conference organizer must return unused support funds
7. Satellite symposiums/luncheon symposiums during conferences are considered part of the conference

### Article 9 (Conference Attendance Support)

1. Companies may support HCPs attending conferences as presenters, chairs, or discussants
2. Support scope: registration, transportation (economy class), accommodation, F&B within limits
3. Domestic conferences: Support through conference organizer delegation
4. Overseas conferences: Support for HCPs delegated by the international conference organizer through domestic academic bodies
5. Prior KMDIA review required

### Article 10 (Product Presentations)

1. Multi-institution presentations: minimum 10 HCP attendees, F&B within limits, at appropriate venues
2. Presentations with accommodation: Prior KMDIA approval required
3. F&B-providing presentations limited to domestic venues
4. Individual institution visits: F&B within limits, max 4 times/month/company/institution

### Article 11 (Education/Training)

1. Training for skills acquisition related to company's medical devices
2. Multi-institution training: accommodation support possible with prior KMDIA approval
3. Single-institution training: accommodation NOT permitted at external venues
4. Overseas training: for devices not imported to Korea, with KMDIA prior review

### Article 12 (Lectures/Consulting)

1. Lecture fees: based on time, within prescribed caps
2. Consulting fees: based on type and time, within caps
3. Annual cap per HCP: 3,000,000 KRW
4. No repeated engagement for same topic without justification
5. Full fee payment before completion prohibited
6. Detailed documentation required

### Article 13 (Clinical Trial Device Provision)

- Clinical trial devices provided/loaned per Medical Devices Act and clinical trial regulations

### Article 14 (Market Research)

1. Survey compensation within limits (50,000 KRW for under 30 min)
2. Must be conducted professionally with documented standards
3. Prior notification required

### Article 15 (Post-Market Surveillance)

1. Case report fee: max 50,000 KRW per report
2. Per Medical Devices Act requirements
3. Prior notification required

### Article 16 (Clinical Activities Other Than PMS)

- Other clinical activities with clear scientific purpose
- Compensation within consulting standards
- Prior KMDIA review required

### Article 17 (Exhibition and Advertising)

1. Exhibition booths at KMDIA-approved conferences
2. Booth rental limits: 5,000,000 KRW, 12 sqm
3. Promotional items: max 10,000 KRW
4. Must comply with Medical Devices Act advertising regulations

## Chapter 3: Reporting and Administration

### Article 18 (Reporting)

1. Companies must file spending reports for economic benefits provided to HCPs
2. Semi-annual reporting cycle
3. Reports submitted through KMDIA system

### Article 19 (Review Committee)

1. KMDIA Fair Competition Code Review Committee handles:
   - Pre-review and approval of activities
   - Complaint investigation
   - Violation determination
   - Penalty recommendations
2. Committee composition: external legal/medical/industry experts

### Article 20 (Penalties)

1. Warning (경고)
2. Corrective order (시정조치명령)
3. Publication of violation (위반사실 공표)
4. Fine: Up to 200% of violation amount
5. Exclusion from KMDIA activities
6. Referral to Fair Trade Commission

### Complaint/Investigation Procedure

1. Receipt of complaint or self-initiated investigation
2. Notification to respondent (10 days to submit defense)
3. Review committee deliberation within 7 days of defense receipt
4. On-site investigation if necessary
5. Decision and notification
6. Appeal process available

## Related Laws

### Fair Trade Act (공정거래법)
- Article 23(1)(3): Prohibition of unfair customer solicitation
- Fair Trade Commission oversight

### Medical Devices Act (의료기기법)
- Article 2: Definition of medical devices
- Article 6, 15: Manufacturing/import licenses
- Article 8: Post-market surveillance
- Article 23-2: Prohibition of economic benefit provision for sales promotion
- Implementing Rules: Permitted economic benefits scope

### Medical Devices Distribution Order Maintenance Rules
- Specific permitted benefits enumerated
- Reporting obligations

### Anti-Graft Act (청탁금지법)
- Applies to HCPs who are also public officials
- Stricter limits may apply:
  - Meals: 30,000 KRW
  - Gifts: 50,000 KRW
  - Congratulatory/condolence: 100,000 KRW
- Lecture fees per government schedule
- When both the Code and Anti-Graft Act apply, use the stricter standard

### Spending Report Guidelines
- 경제적 이익 제공에 따른 지출보고서 작성 가이드라인 (2nd edition)
- Covers reporting requirements for all economic benefits
- Semi-annual submission
- Categories: product presentations, education/training, conferences, lectures/consulting, clinical support, market research, etc.
